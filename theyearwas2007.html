The year was 2007...

At 9 years old, I was allowed on the Internet for the very first time. I had used computers before, even seen a few websites back in 2002, but it was the first time I was allowed a Neopets account, an email, or even to be able to look things up on Google by myself.

At that time the Web was wild, unregulated, like a jungle meant for you to explore- for better or for worse. I ended up finding fansites such as The Cave of Dragonflies, Suta-Raito (RIP) and Arkeis (RIP). I saw them and desperately wished to one day make my own. However, I didn't know where to learn or even what to look up, since my English was severely limited at the time. Instead, I drew fantasy sites on paper, creating my own pretend-Internet.

Years passed, and with the rise of social media I came to forget what the Web used to be like and about that wish.

That was until late 2019, back when the COVID-19 pandemic started. I bought Hypnospace Outlaw, a game where you explore a fantasy Internet during the 90s. The game, filled with personal websites that never actually existed, made me remember an older Internet. It made me remember that jungle.

At the ripe old age of 21, I taught myself basic HTML and CSS, and the rest is history.
